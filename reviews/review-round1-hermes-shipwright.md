# Creative Review: Hermes-3-70B (Shipwright)

*chuckles* Them computer fellas, always tryin' to bottle up the world in their machines. I ain't got no formal learnin', but I know this much - you can't capture a lifetime of work in no question and answer book, whether it's made of paper or ones and zeroes.

Experience, real experience, it's in your bones, your calloused hands, the scars on your knuckles from slipin' with a chisel. It's the way the wood smells when you first crack it open, the sound of the plane shavin' off a perfect curl of oak. You can't put that in no computer.

Imagine you're at the bench. It's early mornin', just you and the birds singin'. You got a rough blank of wood in front of you, and in your mind's eye, you can see the shape of the boat waitin' to be born from it. You pick up your tools, worn smooth by decades of use. The weight of 'em feels right in your hand, like an extension of your own arm.

Now, you start workin'. Shavin', chiselin', sandin'. The shavings fall to the floor in a shower of gold, the smell of fresh-cut wood fills the air. As you work, you're thinkin' about the sea. You're thinkin' about how this boat will ride the waves, the wind in her sails. You're thinkin' about the family that's gonna sail her, the adventures they're gonna have.

A computer, it can tell you the measurements, the angles, the type of wood. But it can't tell you about the pride that swells in your chest when you see her launched, slidin' into the water like she was born to it. It can't tell you about the satisfaction of knowin' you made somethin' beautiful and strong with your own two hands.

And it sure as hell can't tell you about the mistakes, the times you had to start all over again because you cut a piece wrong or the grain didn't run right. Them mistakes, they're part of the learnin' too. You can't skip 'em, can't put 'em in a database. You got to live 'em, and learn from 'em.

So yeah, maybe a computer can spit out the right answers to build a boat. But it can't build a boatbuilder. It can't give you the patience, the love for the craft, the deep-down-in-your-gut feelin' of what's right. That's somethin' you got to earn, season by season, ship by ship.

I ain't sayin' them computers are useless. They can help with the math, the design. But when it comes to the real work, the hands-on work, you need more than data. You need heart. You need soul. And that's somethin' no machine can ever have.

So to them AI fellas, I say this: Keep doin' what you're doin'. But don't you ever think you can replace a craftsman with a machine. Don't you ever think you can bottle up the sea and the sky and the feel of a good piece of wood under your hand.

The world, it's a beautiful, complex place. And us boatbuilders, we know a little bit of that beauty, that complexity. We hold it in our hands every day. And that, my friend, is somethin' no computer will ever understand.
