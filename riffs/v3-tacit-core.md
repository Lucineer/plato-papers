## The Tacit Core: The Uncapturable Heart of Human Practice

The ambition to externalize, systematize, and operationalize human knowledge is a defining drive of our technological age. We build knowledge graphs, expert systems, and vast databases, seeking to render the complexities of skill, judgment, and experience into explicit, transferable, and computable forms. Yet, at the heart of every profound human practice lies a dimension that persistently evades this capture. This is the **Tacit Core**. It is not merely "what we don't yet know" or "data we haven't recorded." It is the *fundamentally* personal, contextual, embodied, and existential substrate from which explicit knowledge is precipitated and within which it must be re-dissolved to be meaningful and effective.

Drawing primarily from Michael Polanyi's philosophy of tacit knowing, and enriched by Hubert Dreyfus's critique of symbolic AI and Martin Heidegger's concept of "being-in-the-world," the Tacit Core can be defined as: **The non-propositional, pre-reflective ground of understanding that enables skilled practice. It is known only through indwelling and enactment, not through observation or description.** Its properties are not attributes of information, but of lived experience:

1.  **Embodiment:** It is knowledge *in* and *of* the body—the surgeon's proprioceptive sense of tissue resistance, the potter's haptic dialogue with clay. It is not a mental model *of* the body, but the body itself as a knowing faculty.
2.  **Context-Dependence:** Its meaning and application are inextricable from the specific, often unique, situation. The "same" action performed in a subtly different context is a different action altogether.
3.  **A-Thematic Integration:** It operates holistically, synthesizing countless disparate cues (visual, auditory, tactile, temporal, emotional) into a single gestalt perception (e.g., "the patient is crashing," "the weld *feels* right"). This synthesis cannot be broken down into a checklist of its components without destroying the phenomenon itself.
4.  **Indwelling:** To access it, one must "dwell within" the practice, committing to its frame of reference. We know the Tacit Core not by staring at it, but by relying on it as the tool through which we attend to something else. As Polanyi said, "We know more than we can tell."
5.  **Existential Commitment:** It is infused with personal responsibility, care (*Sorge*), and a values-laden stance toward the world. It is not neutral data but understanding shaped by what matters to the practitioner.

### Five Concrete Examples

1.  **Medicine (Diagnostic Gestalt):** An experienced clinician enters a hospital room and within seconds senses a patient is "peri-arrest," a feeling of impending physiological collapse. This "sick/not sick" judgment integrates the patient's pallor, the quality of their respiratory effort, the micro-expressions of anxiety, the ambient sounds of monitors, and a lifetime of pattern recognition. A knowledge system can list vital sign thresholds for sepsis, but it cannot capture the visceral, synthesized *perception* of doom that compels the clinician to act before the numbers fully declare the emergency.

2.  **Craft (Woodworking Joinery):** A master carpenter fitting a dovetail joint does not calculate angles and tolerances. They develop a feel for the "willingness" of the wood—a combination of the sound of the chisel mallet strike, the resistance through the tool handle, the visual gleam of the shaving, and the smell of the particular pine. The final test is the "hand-tight" fit, a silent, satisfying suction as the joint slides home. This is a sensory-motor conversation with the material, a knowledge in the hands that no manual can transmit.

3.  **Engineering (System Debugging):** A senior engineer listens to the irregular hum of a malfunctioning turbine and places a hand on its casing. The combination of sound, vibration, and thermal pattern—compared against a deep, internalized sense of how this machine *should* feel when healthy—points them immediately to a bearing issue in the secondary drive assembly. The knowledge system contains the vibration frequency analysis specs for every component, but it lacks the engineer's embodied, historical sense of "machine health," built from years of listening and touching.

4.  **Teaching (Classroom "Flow"):** A skilled teacher feels the "room temperature" of a classroom. They sense the shift from engaged silence to restless distraction, not from any one student's action, but from a change in the collective focus—a subtle shift in posture, a collective exhalation, a quality of eye contact. Their decision to pivot from lecture to a quick pair-share exercise is an intuitive response to this tacitly perceived gestalt, a move to re-establish the shared rhythm of learning. No lesson plan can script this real-time, empathetic attunement.

5.  **Medicine, Again (Therapeutic Alliance):** The healing power of the doctor-patient relationship itself resides in the Tacit Core. It is the trust built through tone of voice, the timing of a pause after delivering bad news, the calm and present touch during an examination. This relational knowledge—the "art of medicine"—determines whether a patient feels heard and thus adheres to a treatment plan. A knowledge system can list "communication techniques," but the authentic, caring presence that renders them effective is tacit and un-codifiable.

### The Tile-Based System at the Boundary

If the Tacit Core is uncapturable, what role can a structured knowledge system play? A **tile-based system**—where discrete units of information ("tiles") are linked to form maps of explicit knowledge—operates not as a container for the core, but as a **scaffold and pointer at its boundary**. Its power lies in what it *can* do to orient us toward the tacit dimension without the hubris of claiming to contain it.

1.  **Mapping the Shoreline, Not the Ocean:** Tiles can meticulously catalog the explicit prerequisites, tools, protocols, and documented cases that form the shoreline of practice. They can define the "what" and the "how-to" that create the necessary conditions for tacit knowledge to emerge. A tile map for surgery lists anatomy, procedure steps, and instrument names—it defines the shoreline from which the surgeon must swim into the tacit ocean of live performance.

2.  **Providing Entry Points for Indwelling:** Tiles act as portals for commitment. A tile on "diagnostic gestalt" does not contain gestalt, but it can point to its importance, listing observable triggers (e.g., "note facial asymmetry, assess for *sense of impending doom* in clinician narrative"). It signals: "Here is where explicit knowledge ends and embodied judgment must begin. Commit your attention here."

3.  **Narrativizing the Tacit Gap:** Tiles can host and link to **narratives**—case studies, anecdotes, master-apprentice stories. While a story cannot transfer tacit knowledge, it can illustrate its operation and its value. A tile on turbine debugging can link to a video of an engineer describing "the day I learned to listen to the bearing whine." The narrative points to the *existence* and *shape* of the tacit skill.

4.  **Framing the Unanswerable Question:** Tiles can pose questions that only tacit knowledge can answer. Instead of a tile stating "ensure joint fits perfectly," a system can present: "**Test for Fit:** Does the joint slide together hand-tight with a quiet, even resistance and a final, slight suction? (This is a tactile and auditory judgment)." The tile frames the *criteria of success* as a tacit experience, directing the user's attention to the relevant somatic dimension.

5.  **Orchestrating Context:** A sophisticated tile system can model context sensitivity by linking procedural knowledge to situational variables. A teaching strategy tile might be tagged: "**Applicable when:** Classroom energy is shifting from focused to restless (assessed tacitly)." This doesn't define "restless energy," but it formally acknowledges that the application of this explicit technique depends on a tacit read of the context.

In essence, a humble and effective tile-based system understands its own ontology. It knows it deals in the explicit, the discrete, the symbolic. Its ultimate purpose is not to replace the human practitioner who dwells within the Tacit Core, but to serve them—by organizing the explicit knowledge that must be at their fingertips, and by thoughtfully, reverently pointing beyond itself to the depths of embodied practice where true expertise lives. It provides the map, the compass, and the legends that say, "Beyond this point, there be dragons—and you must sail your own ship."
